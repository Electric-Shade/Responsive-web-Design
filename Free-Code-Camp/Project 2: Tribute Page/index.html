Ramdhari Singh Dinkar

The writer who traversed the multitude of hindi poetry.

About

  • Ramdhari Singh 'Dinkar' was a prominent writer, poet and essayist of Hindi
  • Dinkar was born on September 24, 1908, in a Bhumihar Brahmin family in Simaria village of Begusarai district of Bihar.
  • He did his BA in History and Political Science from Patna University. He studied Sanskrit, Bengali, English and Urdu intensively.
  • Following his completion of education, he decide to teach while simultaneously writing.

Work

  • He composed poems against social and economic equality and exploitation.
  • As a progressive and humanist poet, he gave a powerful and sharp tone to historical characters and events.
  • His great works include Rashmirathi and Waiting for Parashurama.
  • Except Urvashi, most of Dinkar's works are full of heroic juices. After Bhushan, he is considered to be the best poet of Veer Rasa.
  • Urvashi heaven abandonment is the story of a nymph. At the same time, Kurukshetra is the poetic form of the peace festival of Mahabharata.
  • In the four chapters of Culture, Dinkarji said that despite cultural, linguistic and regional diversities, India is one country.

honor

  • Dinkarji received honors from Kashi Nagari Pracharini Sabha, Government of Uttar Pradesh and Government of India for his work Kurukshetra
  • He was awarded the Sahitya Akademi Award in 1959 for four chapters of culture.
  • Dr. Rajendra Prasad, the first President of India, awarded him the Padma Vibhushan in 1959.
  • On September 30, 1987, on his 13th death anniversary, the then President Zail Singh paid tribute to him.
  • In 1999, the Government of India issued a postage stamp in his memory.
